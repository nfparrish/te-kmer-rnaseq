Build a nextflow bioinformatics pipeline that maps RNAseq data to human or mouse transposable element (TE) family consensus sequences (e.g. use Dfam models) in an error-tolerant manner, and then quantifies expression of k-mers of k~50 (decomposed in sliding windows from the mapped reads and their read-pairs) relative to the RNA sequencing depth. Then, k-mers will be clustered (k-means) based on normalized expression to determine which k-mers are likely reflective of the same TE (defined by sequence, not by locus) transcript. Because of the repetitive nature of TEs and the inadequacy of reference genome to capture TE insertional polymorphisms, this tool will be used to measure heterogeneity in TE sequence-specific expression across different individuals. Specifically, k-mer cluster expression levels will be associated with genetic variation to determine factors causally impacting expression of specific TE sequences. If possible, download human RNAseq datasets from https://www.ebi.ac.uk/biostudies/arrayexpress/studies/E-MTAB-3788 to validate the pipeline.
